Consolidation Region V2

Overview

The Consolidation Region V2 indicator is a sophisticated tool designed to identify range-bound market conditions using the Ehlers Dynamic Smoothed Moving Average (EDSMA) methodology. This advanced system combines trend analysis with volatility measurements to accurately detect consolidation phases.

By utilizing ATR-based bands and slope filtering, the indicator effectively distinguishes between trending and consolidating market conditions, providing traders with clear visual confirmation of range-bound opportunities.

EDSMA Methodology

Ehlers Dynamic Smoothed Moving Average

The EDSMA is an advanced moving average developed by John Ehlers that adapts to market conditions by adjusting its smoothing factor based on price velocity and volatility.

EDSMA[i] = α × Price[i] + (1-α) × EDSMA[i-1]
where α = 2 / (Period + 1) × Adaptive Factor

The adaptive factor adjusts based on market conditions, making the average more responsive during volatile periods and smoother during consolidation phases.

Dynamic Trend Assessment

The EDSMA continuously evaluates price momentum and adjusts its sensitivity accordingly:

  • High Velocity: Increased responsiveness to capture trend changes
  • Low Velocity: Enhanced smoothing to filter noise
  • Volatility Adjustment: Automatic calibration based on market conditions

Implementation Details

Detection Algorithm

The indicator employs a multi-stage detection process:

  1. EDSMA Calculation: Dynamic smoothing based on market velocity
  2. ATR Band Generation: Volatility-adjusted boundary calculation
  3. Slope Analysis: Trend strength evaluation and filtering
  4. Consolidation Validation: Minimum duration and stability checks

Range Boundary Logic

Consolidation zones are identified when:

  • Price remains within ATR bands for specified duration
  • EDSMA slope stays below threshold value
  • No significant breakout signals detected
  • Volatility remains within normal range
