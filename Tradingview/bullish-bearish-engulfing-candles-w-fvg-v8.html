This advanced system identifies bullish and bearish engulfing patterns while simultaneously analyzing Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) to provide high-probability trading opportunities.

By combining traditional technical analysis with institutional trading concepts, this indicator helps traders identify moments when retail sentiment conflicts with smart money positioning, creating optimal entry conditions for informed traders.

Fair Value Gap Integration

Fair Value Gaps represent imbalances in the market where institutional orders create price inefficiencies. The indicator identifies these gaps and tracks their interaction with engulfing patterns to create high-probability trading scenarios.

Key Innovation

Detection Logic

Bullish Engulfing Criteria

  • Current candle opens below or at the previous candle's low
  • Current candle closes above the previous candle's high
  • Current candle body is significantly larger than previous candle body
  • Previous candle must be bearish (red/down candle)
  • Current candle must be bullish (green/up candle)
  • Volume should show increase (configurable)
  • Pattern occurs at or near swing low (if swing detection enabled)

Bearish Engulfing Criteria

  • Current candle opens above or at the previous candle's high
  • Current candle closes below the previous candle's low
  • Current candle body is significantly larger than previous candle body
  • Previous candle must be bullish (green/up candle)
  • Current candle must be bearish (red/down candle)
  • Volume should show increase (configurable)
  • Pattern occurs at or near swing high (if swing detection enabled)

Fair Value Gap Analysis

FVG Identification Process

Fair Value Gaps are identified when there is a price imbalance between consecutive candles, indicating areas where institutional orders have created inefficiencies that the market will likely revisit.

Bullish FVG

Occurs when the low of the current candle is higher than the high of the candle two periods ago, creating an upward gap that represents unfilled demand.

Bearish FVG

Occurs when the high of the current candle is lower than the low of the candle two periods ago, creating a downward gap that represents unfilled supply.

FVG-Engulfing Confluence

The most powerful signals occur when engulfing patterns interact with existing Fair Value Gaps. This confluence suggests that:

  • Institutional orders are being executed at significant levels
  • Market inefficiencies are being addressed
  • Strong directional momentum is likely to continue
  • Retail traders are positioned opposite to smart money

Confluence Trading

When an engulfing pattern forms within or near a Fair Value Gap, it creates a high-probability trading scenario. The gap provides the "why" (institutional imbalance) while the engulfing pattern provides the "when" (momentum shift).
